Buy It

Now you know about FIGnition: it's the only computer so simple you can build it, code it and understand it! So why not buy it?

It's easy to buy a FIGnition; over 1000 have been shipped since May 2011 including around 600 FUZE kits since April 2012! FIGnition FUZE is available directly from this site, and is now also available with bundled extras from RS Components as FIGnition inFUZE!

You can also order a combo package of FIGnition with FIGkeys (the FIGnition PS/2 Interface) and save on postage! Note: if you do this, the earliest estimated shipping date will be mid-January 2014 and both kits will be delivered together. Your FIGnition will work independently of FIGkeys; you can build each kits one at a time.

Construct It

Although it will cost you a bit more in effort and money, you can construct a FIGnition using nothing more than basic soldering equipment the raw set of components and veroboard.

You'll find the hardware and software for FIGnition is fully opened-sourced on GitHub and includes the source code for the keyboard driver, the startup screen, the video generator; the Forth virtual machine; the Forth compiler; the Flash disk driver as well as the Osmond X circuit layout and the circuit layout for the Veroboard version using my open-source stripboard designer: Stripes.

Education

To date, over 200 FIGnition kits have been ordered by UK educational institutions including schools and colleges and Universities for STEM* clubs and workshops. For business reasons it may be inconvenient to buy through Paypal or the shopping cart and in these cases it's best to contact me and then we'll follow a more formal purchasing procedure:
  1. A formal order from the school or college for the kit(s).
  2. An invoice from me, which includes the payment method details.
  3. Payment from the school or college.
  4. A delivery from me (with a receipt sent by email and in the package).
For bulk orders I can easily supply tens of kits at short notice and hundreds of kits within a few weeks (for example, October 2012's Strathclyde University order of 160 kits took three weeks to deliver).

*(Science, Technology, Engineering and Mathematics).
